How I Work

A repeatable process

Not a rigid formula — but the stages every project moves through, in roughly this order.

01 / Discover

Talk to real users

Interviews, surveys, and support tickets — finding the actual problem before sketching anything.

02 / Define

Frame the problem

Turn research into a clear brief: who it's for, what success looks like, what's out of scope.

03 / Design

Wireframe to prototype

Low-fidelity first, then high-fidelity flows tested with real users before any code is written.

04 / Deliver

Hand off & iterate

Specs, components, and documentation engineers can actually build from — then refine post-launch.

About

A bit more context

Where I've been, and the tools I reach for most.

I'm a UI/UX designer who started out sketching app flows on the back of notebook margins and ended up doing it for a living. I care most about the small decisions — copy on an empty state, the timing of a transition, whether a form actually respects someone's time.

My work usually sits at the intersection of research and craft: I'm comfortable running a usability study on a Monday and refining 4px spacing in a component on a Tuesday. Both matter.

Outside of client work, I spend time reverse-engineering interfaces I admire, and slowly building a type system I'm happy with.
